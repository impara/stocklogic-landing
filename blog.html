Why Your Shopify Bundle Inventory is a Mess (and How to Fix It)

Bundle inventory becomes difficult when one sellable product depends on several component quantities, especially when those quantities differ by location.

If bundle availability is maintained separately from component stock, every sale, return, transfer, or manual adjustment can create another mismatch.

Where bundle inventory drifts

Shopify's native Bundles app is a good fit for many straightforward catalogs. Other setups need different recipe rules, backend-only inventory handling, or location-specific calculations.

A common failure mode is maintaining a separate bundle quantity instead of calculating availability from the component SKUs. The numbers can diverge after component sales, purchase-order receipts, returns, or warehouse transfers.

Common symptoms:

  • A bundle remains available after a required component reaches zero.
  • Stock at one warehouse overwrites bundle availability at another.
  • Staff repeatedly correct bundle quantities by hand.

Keep inventory logic separate from storefront presentation

Some merchants need bundle widgets or build-your-own experiences in the storefront. Others already have the product pages they want and only need inventory synchronization. A backend-only tool avoids adding theme code when no storefront bundle builder is required.

StockLogic keeps the original Shopify order intact. It does not add hidden products, component lines, or zero-value line items to customer orders.

The location-aware calculation

Suppose a Starter Kit uses 3 bottles, 2 towels, and 1 bag. Warehouse A can build 6 kits, while Warehouse B can build 2. A single store-wide bundle quantity cannot represent both locations correctly.

StockLogic calculates the maximum bundle quantity separately at each enabled Shopify-managed location. When Shopify assigns an order to Warehouse A, component deductions are applied there rather than across every warehouse. Moves and split fulfillment assignments are reconciled from Shopify's current routing.

Important limitation: StockLogic does not adjust third-party or fulfillment-service locations. It detects those assignments and stops without changing stock.

For each location, StockLogic calculates the Maximum Bundle Quantity (MBQ) from the limiting component. If a recipe needs 3 bottles and only 2 remain at that location, the bundle availability there becomes 0.

What StockLogic handles

  • Fulfillment-aware deductions: Components follow Shopify's assigned location, including supported moves and splits.
  • Loop-safe updates: Concurrency checks reduce the risk of one inventory update overwriting a newer value.
  • Visible activity: The activity log records the component, location, calculation, and operations that need attention.
